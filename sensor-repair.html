Common Problems

Signs Your Garage Door Sensors Need Repair

Safety sensors are a federal requirement on all garage doors made after 1993. When they fail, your door either won't close or becomes unsafe. Here's what to look for.

Door Reverses Immediately

Closes then immediately reverses back up — the most classic sign of misaligned sensors. The infrared beam is interrupted before the door reaches the ground.

Door Won't Close at All

Opener runs but the door refuses to move downward. When sensors aren't communicating, the opener defaults to a safety lockout and won't allow closing.

Sensor Light is Blinking or Off

Each sensor has an LED light — one should be solid green, one solid amber/yellow. Blinking or missing lights indicate a misalignment or wiring issue.

Remote or Keypad Stops Working

Remote worked fine then stopped responding. Could be a battery, a lost programming, or a sensor communication issue — we diagnose and fix all three.

Door Only Closes Holding Wall Button

If the door closes only when you hold the wall button but not with the remote, your sensors are misaligned — the held-button mode bypasses sensor detection.

New Remote or Keypad Not Working

Replaced your remote or added a keypad but can't get it to work? We program all LiftMaster, Chamberlain, and Genie remotes and keypads on the spot.

Our Process

How We Diagnose & Fix Your Sensors

Sensor issues are fast to diagnose and usually quick to fix — most sensor calls are resolved in under an hour, often for much less than you'd expect.

1

Test the Full System

We test sensor alignment, LED status, wiring connections, and remote signal — to pinpoint whether it's alignment, debris, wiring, or a failed sensor unit.

2

Clean & Realign Sensors

Most sensor issues are caused by a slight knock or debris on the lens. We clean both sensors and realign them precisely until both LEDs show solid lights.

3

Replace if Needed

If a sensor is damaged, corroded, or its wiring is faulty, we replace it on the spot. We carry replacement sensors for LiftMaster, Chamberlain, and Genie in our vans.

4

Program Remotes & Keypads

While on-site we also program any remotes or keypads you need set up — all brands, all models, all in one visit so you don't need a second appointment.
